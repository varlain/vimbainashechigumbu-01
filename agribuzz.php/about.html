AGRIBUSINESS AND MARKETING /FARM MANAGEMENT BRANCH

INTRODUCTION

What is Agribusiness and Marketing/Farm Management?

The Branch of Agribusiness and Marketing /Farm Management is a specialised service unit tailored to provide a professional agricultural, management, agribusiness and project management services to the department, government, farming community and the general agricultural industry and trade. It provides an important link between the farming community, government, research, trade and industry.

Specialists in the branch require a background in agricultural economics and their responsibilities include collecting, analyzing and disseminating information on economic aspects of the farm to farmers, extension workers and policy-makers. Farm management extension is concerned with the development of management strategies and skills among farmers for improved decision-making in the use of resources and linking farmers to markets. With the increasing market-orientation of farming, the decisions taken by farmers are more complex; for farms to compete they must be run as a business. This creates a demand for specialized extension support. To be successful farmers need the skills to produce what the market wants and what satisfies consumers.

The key areas of operational interest are:

  1. Farm business planning and management
  2. Agribusiness development
  3. Marketing of agricultural produce
  4. Farm enterprise and systems viability
  5. Farm Management Data Bases
  6. Work studies
  7. Labour and Equipment Planning
  8. Value chain analysis and development and value addition
  9. National agricultural development projects planning
  10. Project and Programme Monitoring and Evaluation
  11. Socio-Economic Research and Techniques
  12. Social appraisal
  13. Participatory methods, technology uptake and communication
  14. Updating of management and economic tools for extension purposes
  15. Staff training and materials development
  16. Gender training and development

`To facilitate the effective provision of services the branch should have a minimum compliment of 6 posts at head office and 8 at provincial level (one at each province). Agricultural economists provide cross cutting specialists services to all sections in the department and the ministry at large hence the need for the 6 posts at head office and one at each province. The specialist at the province will be an all-rounder and some of the work will depend on the particular province.

The following are the combined summarised job descriptions

Farm Management/ Marketing/ Value chain/ Agribusiness Development/ Monitoring and Evaluation

  1. Regularly and timely assess and report on viability of nationally important enterprises.
  2. Develop and maintain accessible farm management data bases and M & E databases.
  3. Develop, update and maintain different enterprise budgets.
  4. Appraisal of the economics of a range of farming systems/ enterprises and practices.
  5. Provide an impartial farm viability assessment and project planning reports to interested parties on a request basis including subdivision proposals and determination of criteria thereof.
  6. Assess and report on cost benefit relationships of alternative and new technologies and practices.
  7. Provide on a needs basis, training for staff and other agencies in areas of Farming as a Business, Marketing Management, data analysis and sampling techniques and data analysis (including development of learning materials, facilitate sessions, course assessments and evaluations)
  8. Develop various practical field manuals for both staff and farmers
  9. Provide detailed farm labour and equipment planning reports on request and provide specialised trainings on labour and equipment planning
  10. Carry out socio-economic for different projects including adoption studies.
  11. Carry out work study research for different enterprises and update different usage rates
  12. Carry out value chain analysis for the different value chains and inform clients for informed decision making.
  13. Identify value chain opportunities, notably by mapping and evaluating existing supply chains, and recommend additional priority opportunities for value chain development;
  14. 14. Outline value chain opportunities by developing project summaries and market analysis reports;
  15. Assist entrepreneurs and prospective investors by providing advice and technical support, including business case development, economic viability analysis, risk assessments and feasibility of value chain projects;
  16. Assist in the formation of value chain partnerships and networks, notably by assessing and reviewing existing value chain priorities, stakeholders and projects;
  17. Develop and maintain a database of companies and agencies involved in supply chains and value chain activities;
  18. Establish trends in markets and inform government for informed policy direction.
  19. Provide staff and farmers with specific market information (quality requirements, potential markets, consumer preferences and marketing channels)
  20. Analyse and review different markets and link farmers to potential markets
  21. Training and facilitate farmers on carrying out market research
  22. Provide financial and economic analysis for various farm and community and national projects.
  23. Identify opportunities for diversification and value adding in rural areas.
  24. Evaluate investment opportunities at farm level.
  25. Prepare papers on issues in economics and agricultural development.
  26. Develop project proposals for funding for the department.
  27. Assist farmers in developing their project proposals/ business plans/ 5 year development plans for funding including special projects (requirements for medium and long term loan applications and applications for land).
  28. Developing the monitoring and evaluation frameworks for the different projects
  29. Evaluating agricultural programmes and/or projects implemented by the department, and other organisations to contribute to the improvement of current and future project implementation.
  30. Conducting socio-economic baseline surveys of agricultural projects to provide benchmarks for subsequent evaluations.
  31. Identify, plan, implement and evaluate projects with farmers and other stakeholders using participatory methods to cultivate sense of ownership among stakeholders thereby ensuring sustainability of projects.
  32. Training Agritex technical staff on Project Planning and Appraisal (project preparation and planning to equip them with the necessary skills required to identify, analyze, prepare, monitor and evaluate projects).
  33. Developing logical frameworks and M&E formats for projects being implemented by the department, and other organisations to effectively implement, monitor and evaluate projects.
  34. Advise departmental staff on questionnaire designing, administration and data analysis
  35. Prepare and present technical papers for workshops and conferences within and outside the department.
